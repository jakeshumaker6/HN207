What is Gerrymandering?

Gerrymandering is the manipulation of congressional districts in a specific way to favor one party or candidate over the over.

Gerrymandering in 1812

In 1812, the Boston Gazette first used the word "Gerrymandering" in a political cartoon.

Gerrymandering through the years

Over the years, gerrymandering has increased all over the United States.

Examples of Gerrymandering

North Carolina, 12th District. 97.09 rating for Gerrymandering.

Which party tends to Gerrymander?

Both parties gerrymander, restructuring districts can benefit both parties.

Mathematics behind Gerrymandering

The mathematics behind gerrymandering is that each congressional district needs to have similar populations, and each party wants to win the most congressional districts they can for the most representation.

Usage of Mathematics for Fair Congressional Redistricting

We can use math to fairly structure districts in a way without bias from political parties.
